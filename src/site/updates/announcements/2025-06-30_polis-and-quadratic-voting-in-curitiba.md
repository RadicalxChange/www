Hello RadxChanger,

As polarization intensifies, many cities today struggle to meaningfully engage their communities. Traditional participation methods often fall short, treating engagement as a one-way activity—like ticking a box—rather than facilitating authentic dialogue. RxC is dedicated to changing this, helping communities build trust and identify common ground with platforms that foster nuanced, inclusive conversations.

[![](/images/announcements/newsletters/2025-06-polis-and-quadratic-voting-in-curitiba/57fd6c77-1ef9-6813-6131-3e8448bd32f6.png)](https://bit.ly/rxccwb0625)

Earlier this month, we held a capacity-building workshop in Curitiba, Brazil, where public servants dived into hands-on sessions with innovative tools like Pol.is and Quadratic Voting. Participants experienced first-hand how these methods can foster healthier dialogues at scale. Following the workshop, our team met with city officials in Curitiba to explore deeper collaboration and integration of digital democracy tools in local governance.

![](/images/announcements/newsletters/2025-06-polis-and-quadratic-voting-in-curitiba/83bd0709-def5-e3b0-edf5-973cc33423a9.png)

If you are connected to a city or organization that could benefit from similar support and would like to explore how to make it happen, do not hesitate to reach out! :)

## From Around the Pluriverse

→ [The Case Against Planetary Governance](https://www.combinationsmag.com/the-case-against-planetary-governance/): An interview with Wolfgang Streeck in [Combinations Magazine](https://www.combinationsmag.com/). Streeck believes that the future of democracy lies not in newfangled structures of planetary governance, but in a recuperation of the nation state’s lost capacities.  
  
→ [An Adult World, Without Adults](https://thedispatch.com/article/internet-children-parents-porn-virtual-safeguards/): An excerpt from Clare Morell's new book, _The Tech Exit: A Practical Guide to Freeing Kids and Teens from Smartphones._  
  
→ [Building A Prosocial Media Ecosystem](https://www.noemamag.com/building-a-prosocial-media-ecosystem/): An essay in Noema by Glen Weyl, Audrey Tang and Jacob Mchangama. There’s never been a better time to make social media what it always should have been: a tool to strengthen rather than strip-mine the social fabric.  
  
→ [Public AI Seminar: Season 3](https://publicai.network/seminar): A research seminar organized by Joshua Tan, Alek Tarkowski and B Cavello to study public AI and other forms of public interest AI. Guests include Mat Dryhurst, Ted Chiang, EM Lewis-Jong, Martin Tisné, Gideon Lichfield and Kim Stanley Robinson.  
  
→ [Here’s How To Share AI’s Future Wealth](https://www.noemamag.com/heres-how-to-share-ais-future-wealth/): An essay in Noema by Saffron Huang and Sam Manning. Advanced AI threatens to increase inequality and concentrate power, but we can proactively distribute AI’s benefits to foster a just and inclusive economy before it’s too late.  
  
→ [On Abundance, by Ezra Klein and Derek Thompson, with h/t to Henry Farrell](https://bsky.app/profile/glenweyl.bsky.social/post/3lqsmggyc7c24/quotes): A Bluesky thread by Glen Weyl. We cannot "get past" process. We must improve on it.
