Hello RadicalxChanger,

We're less than two weeks away from [**The Future of Digital Democracy: Learning with Ukraine**](https://www.radicalxchange.org/events/2025-ukraine/), and we're thrilled to announce some exciting additions to our speaker lineup.  
  
**Takahiro Anno**, Member of Japan's House of Councillors and founder of [Team Mirai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Team_Mirai), will join us to share how he's pioneered [broad listening](https://www.combinationsmag.com/the-art-of-broad-listening/) in Japanese politics. His work demonstrates how technology can bridge the gap between citizens and policymakers, creating new pathways for democratic participation.  
  
**Zarah Bruhn**, Social Innovation Officer at [SPRIN-D](https://www.sprind.org/en) in Germany, will share insights on how governments can institutionalize innovation and build partnerships across civil society and business to transform citizen participation. Drawing on Taiwan's successful transformation of public trust in democracy, she'll explore what Europe can learn from these cross-sector experiments.  
  
[View the full speaker lineup on our event page](https://luma.com/0iyrzr14). From Ukraine's frontline innovations to groundbreaking experiments in Taiwan, Japan, Scotland, Brazil and Germany, this is a rare opportunity to learn alongside the leaders shaping the future of democracy.

## Join the Mutualis Network

Our event last week on [Foundations for the Digital Commons](https://www.radicalxchange.org/events/2025-maine/) was a big hit, and one of the projects emerging from it is the **Mutualis network** – a group of legal practitioners, students and fellow-travelers that supports innovative legal arrangements, like data coalitions and unconventional cooperatives, through open-source templates. Reach out if you want to be looped in!

## From Around the Pluriverse

→ [Audrey Tang wins 2025 Right Livelihood Award](https://rightlivelihood.org/the-change-makers/find-a-laureate/audrey-tang/): “For advancing the social use of digital technology to empower citizens, renew democracy and heal divides.”  
  
→ [C](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xeATYZi1AF4)[an technology save democracy? Taiwan's cyber ambassador Audrey Tang thinks so](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xeATYZi1AF4): Audrey sits down with Margaret Hoover on PBS's Firing Line to discuss how technology can strengthen democratic institutions and counter authoritarianism.  
  
→ [GreenEarth Social](https://www.greenearth.social/p/introducing-greenearth): An open-source project led by Glen Weyl, Jonathan Stray and Renee DiResta building AI-driven, user-controllable feed algorithms for ATProto, letting people customize their social media experience through natural language prompts.  
  
→ Malik Lakoubay represented RxC at the Paris Peace Forum's [Conference on Information Integrity](https://ifpim.org/news/29-governments-join-forces-in-defense-of-access-to-free-and-reliable-information-worldwide), where he promoted our work on collective bargaining as a critical tool for information integrity.
