Greetings RadicalxChanger,

Last month RadicalxChange partnered with the Web3 Institute in Ukraine to host **[The Future of Digital Democracy: Learning with Ukraine](https://luma.com/0iyrzr14)**, bringing together participants from around the world to explore how communities are strengthening democracy under pressure.   
  
        [![](/images/announcements/newsletters/2025-12-lessons-from-learning-with-ukraine/7df4c616-b9c6-f403-227f-8075c98752a9.png)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zwaIZcS9cW8&list=PLV_W9wE6WVDhmBqW7guOKz--t_-oGS0eB&pp=gAQB)           
  
Ukraine's democratic transformation has been remarkable. In just two years, the country jumped from 57th to 1st in the UN E-Participation Index. [Katia Lukicheva](https://youtu.be/3YNuAGlcvDk) shared how Ukraine democratized health funding through hyperlocal participation, [Oleksandra Radchenko](https://youtu.be/3PuK8_t2JwU) showed how participatory budgeting now engages citizens in hundreds of communities, often exceeding election turnout, and [Oleksii Zhmerenetskyi](https://youtu.be/8RJrj-BS2dc) spoke about the centrality of digital services and tools like Diia and a continued focus on the infrastructure of decentralization and digital democracy to Ukraine's long-term strategic planning.  
  
Beyond Ukraine, [Audrey Tang](https://youtu.be/zwaIZcS9cW8?si=dvtvmJt4UGZdE62K) demonstrated how AI can deepen democracy, [Takahiro Anno](https://youtu.be/7m9j1CSWE54?si=gvZcFzVJIa5AoNEN) shared Team Mirai's broad listening work in Japan, and [Zarah Bruhn](https://youtu.be/n_IakRjmsJ4?si=W4b5Whs622IurhW7) outlined plans at SPRIN-D (Germany's Federal Agency for Disruptive Innovation) to launch civic hackathons experimenting with some of the tools in our space – more on this soon!  
  
Drawing on these insights, we're developing the Resilient Democracy Toolkit, a practical guide translating lessons from digital identity, deliberation, and decentralized governance into actionable guidance for organizers worldwide. If your organization would like to partner, get in touch at [info@radicalxchange.org](mailto:info@radicalxchange.org).  
  
Missed the event? The talks are now on [Youtube](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLV_W9wE6WVDhmBqW7guOKz--t_-oGS0eB). Thanks to Max Semenchuk and the Web3 Institute in Ukraine for their partnership!

## Results from the Agora Consultation

Over the last two months, 23 community members contributed 483 votes and insights on [Agora](https://agoracitizen.network/feed/conversation/4OcpxQ) about our values and principles. The results revealed a strong foundation: we value openness and experimentation, and agree that progress requires direction, not just speed.   
  
A productive tension emerged around how pluralism applies in today's political climate. Two equal-sized groups formed distinct positions: "**Pluralists**" who argue we should take stronger stances to defend democratic norms, and "**Pragmatists**" who believe maintaining neutrality preserves space for diverse experimentation.  
  
This tension reflects a crucial distinction: **defending the foundations that make pluralism possible is non-negotiable, but within those foundations we must remain radically open to engaging with all democratic perspectives, including those that challenge prevailing orthodoxies**. The question isn't whether to take stances, but which stances preserve the conditions for genuine democratic plurality.
