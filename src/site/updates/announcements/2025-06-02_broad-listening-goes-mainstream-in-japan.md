Hi RadxChanger,

Last year, when Takahiro Anno decided to run for Governor of Tokyo, the idea of [broad listening](https://www.combinationsmag.com/the-art-of-broad-listening/) was very niche in Japan. But as Glen and Audrey found out on their most recent visit, for the Japanese launch of the [Plurality book](https://plurality.net/), the idea has been spreading widely and rapidly since then. So much so that all of Japan's major political parties, including the LDP, have already embraced it and are now testing and deploying it at scale **–** fostering quicker, fairer and more engaging citizen participation.  
  
While other nations struggle with democratic gridlock or retreat into nationalism and authoritarianism, Japan now stands uniquely positioned to develop and share with the world a positive vision of what democracy could be in the 21st century. We hope they have the courage to continue advancing and investing in that vision, at a time when we all desperately need it.

## RxC EU's First Retreat

Our EU chapter leaders got together last week for a 3-day retreat at [Domaine de Manzac](https://www.manzac.com/) in the south of France. Coming from six countries across Europe, they exchanged many ideas and even made plans to better coordinate the RxC EU chapters going forward based on _Community Wisdom Gardening_, an idea shared by Charles Blass.

## RadicalxChange(s) | Jonathon Keats: Experimental Philosopher

In this conversation Matt and Jonathon discuss the philosophy of timekeeping. They consider the connectedness and the alienation of being on universal atomic time, the promise of alternative systems such as the river clock, and how different notions of timekeeping influence our understanding of democracy and nature.
