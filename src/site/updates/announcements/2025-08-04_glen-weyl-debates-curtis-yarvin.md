Hello RadxChanger,

On September 4th in New York City, Glen Weyl will step onto the debate stage to confront one of the most influential thinkers of our time. The topic: "Should the U.S. be ruled by a CEO dictator?" His opponent: Curtis Yarvin, the self-described monarchist and founder of the neo-reactionary movement (NRx).  
  
Since RadicalxChange was born, we [have](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2018/12/31/ethereums-new-radical-glen-weyl-isnt-vitalik-but-hes-its-next-best-hope) [called](https://www.radicalxchange.org/media/blog/sovereign-nonsense/) [out](https://www.radicalxchange.org/media/blog/political-ideologies-for-the-21st-century/) Yarvin's vision of the future as a serious threat to human freedom and democracy. Unfortunately, such warnings have proven prescient, as NRx has gone on to capture the imagination of some of the most powerful people in the world, including [Elon Musk](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/07/09/us/politics/elon-musk-curtis-yarvin-third-party.html) and [JD Vance](https://www.vox.com/policy-and-politics/23373795/curtis-yarvin-neoreaction-redpill-moldbug).  
  
We see this debate as a unique opportunity to push back against Yarvin's mistaken ideas and to champion a more hopeful, democratic future. We hope you will join us in supporting Glen next month.

## From Around the Pluriverse

→ [In Defense of Secular Religions](https://www.combinationsmag.com/in-defense-of-secular-religions/): An essay by Thomas Moynihan in [Combinations Magazine](https://www.combinationsmag.com/). Modern ideals of scientific and moral progress promise that rational inquiry can cleanse our beliefs of worldly bias. The history of those ideals suggests that this is neither possible nor desirable.  
  
→ [Cloudflare Wades into the Battle Over AI Consent and Compensation](https://www.techpolicy.press/cloudflare-wades-into-the-battle-over-ai-consent-and-compensation/): An article by Courtney Radsch in Tech Policy Press. "Cloudflare’s move introduces a consent-based architecture for AI crawling... But... it opens up a new set of questions about governance, accountability, and concentration of power."  
  
→ [Saving Journalism with Sectoral Data Bargaining](https://mydata.org/2025/07/28/saving-journalism-with-sectoral-data-bargaining/): A MyData Global blog post by Matt Prewitt, Jack Henderson & Malik Lakoubay.  
  
→ [EU Data Union Strategy: RxC Contribution](https://www.radicalxchange.org/media/blog/eu-data-union-strategy/): An EU policy brief by Matt Prewitt, Jack Henderson & Malik Lakoubay.
